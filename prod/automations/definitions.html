    Terminology¶

    Welcome to the "Terminology" chapter of the Backendless Automations User Guide. Familiarizing yourself with the terminology used throughout this product is crucial for understanding the basic concepts, making your exploration and use of the system as smooth and efficient as possible. Let's dive into the key terms you'll encounter as you navigate Backendless Automations.

    Basic Concepts¶

    • Flow: A Flow is the core element of Backendless Automations, representing a sequence of triggers and actions designed to automate a business process, pipeline, or service function. The flexibility of the system allows you to tailor each Flow to meet diverse goals and purposes.

    • Flow Version: Each Flow can have multiple variations, known as versions, which may include changes or modifications. You can clone an existing version to create a new one. However, only one version can be in the runnable (LIVE) state at any time, and modifications are not allowed on a running version. To update, you must clone it, make changes, stop the LIVE version, and then launch the modified version.

    Components of a Flow¶

    • Block: A general term for Triggers, Actions, Transformers, and Conditions. These are the building blocks of your Flows.

    • Trigger: A type of Block that initializes a Flow when a specific event occurs, such as a user interaction in an app. Triggers put the Flow in a waiting state until the event takes place, then the Flow resumes execution.

    • Action: Actions are blocks that perform specific operations within a Flow, like sending emails, generating PDFs, or executing API services. They can accept input parameters and generate results for use in subsequent stages of the Flow.

    • Transformer: This block type is tasked with data transformation, supporting a wide array of operations like data extraction, manipulation, and conversion. Transformers can be linked together to facilitate complex data transformations.

    • Condition: Conditions evaluate logical expressions to direct the Flow's path based on the outcome (true or false). They play a crucial role in adding decision-making capabilities to your Flows.

    • Groups: Special block types that can contain other blocks. Backendless Automations supports two group types: Triggers Group and Actions Group, designed to manage execution flow within grouped triggers or actions, respectively.

    • Flow Editor: The graphical interface for creating, editing, testing, running, and analyzing Flows. It's where your automation ideas come to life.

    • Test Mode: A feature of the Flow Editor that allows you to debug Flows by configuring test data and executing individual blocks, ensuring each part of your Flow works as intended.

    • SLA Condition: Elements within a service level agreement (SLA) that specify performance or operational standards. Backendless Automations monitors these conditions to ensure compliance.

    • Non-compliance Rule: Defined within an SLA, these rules outline the actions to be taken if a condition approaches or reaches a predefined threshold, ensuring adherence to SLA standards.

    • SLA Configurator: A section within the Flow Editor dedicated to setting up SLA conditions and non-compliance rules, essential for managing performance and reliability standards of your Flows.

    Understanding these terms is the first step in unlocking the full potential of Backendless Automations. As you become more familiar with these concepts, you'll find yourself more comfortable navigating and utilizing the system to automate and optimize your business processes.
